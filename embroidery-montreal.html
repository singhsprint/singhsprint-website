Embroidery that looks like Day 1 for years

What embroidery is best for, when we'd suggest printing instead, and what goes into a stitch that lasts.

Best for logos and uniforms

Embroidery is the premium choice for left-chest logos, crests, hats and corporate uniforms. The raised, stitched finish reads as quality and outlasts any print - ideal for staff wear that gets worn and washed constantly.

When we'd suggest printing

Embroidery isn't right for everything. Large full-colour graphics, photo art, gradients and fine detail are better printed (DTG or DTF). If your design is big or photographic, we'll tell you and quote print instead - honest advice over upsell.

Digitizing done in-house

Every logo is digitized - converted into a stitch file - before it goes on a machine. We handle that in-house and include it, then sample your stitched logo so you approve the look before the full run.

FAQ

Embroidery questions we get

How durable is embroidery compared to printing?

Very. Commercial-grade embroidery thread survives 50+ wash cycles with no visible degradation - it won't crack or peel the way some prints eventually do. That's why we recommend it by default for uniforms, polos and hats.

Do you charge extra for digitizing my logo?

Digitizing - converting your logo into a stitch file - is included with your order. We do it in-house and keep the file on record so reorders are fast and consistent.

What can you embroider?

Polos, caps and hats, hoodies, jackets, beanies, aprons, bags and most uniform garments. For very large or photographic designs we'll recommend printing instead, since embroidery suits logos and text best.

Is there a minimum for embroidery?

No minimum. We'll embroider a single polo or a few hundred. Per-unit price drops with quantity; reorders use your saved stitch file.

Can I bring my own garments to embroider?

Yes. Bring your own polos, hats or jackets and we'll decorate them at the same per-piece rate. We can also source blanks from S&S Activewear, SanMar and Canadian-made suppliers.
