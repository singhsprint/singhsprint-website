4. Consent

We collect and use your information with your consent, which you give by submitting a form, placing an order, or otherwise contacting us. For non-essential cookies and tracking technologies (analytics and advertising), we ask for your consent through a banner before they activate, and you can change your choice at any time using the "Cookie preferences" link in the footer. You may withdraw consent at any time (subject to legal or contractual limits) by contacting our Privacy Officer.

5. Cookies and tracking technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies. Strictly necessary cookies are always on; analytics and advertising technologies (Google Analytics, Google Ads, Meta Pixel) load only after you accept them. Full details are in our Cookie Policy.

7. How long we keep it

We keep personal information only as long as needed for the purposes above or as required by law (for example, tax records are kept for the periods required by Canadian and Quebec tax rules). When no longer needed, we securely delete or anonymize it.

8. How we protect it

We use reasonable security measures, including encryption in transit (HTTPS), access controls, row-level database security, and limiting access to staff who need it. Raw IP addresses are stored only as a one-way daily hash. No method is perfectly secure, but we work to protect your information and will notify affected individuals and the Commission d'acces a l'information (CAI) of any confidentiality incident that presents a risk of serious injury, as required by law.

9. Your rights

Subject to legal limits, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you;
  • Have inaccurate information corrected;
  • Withdraw your consent or ask us to stop using your information;
  • Request deletion or de-indexing where applicable;
  • Receive your computerized information in a structured, commonly used format (data portability).

To exercise these rights, contact our Privacy Officer at sales@singhsprint.com. We will respond within 30 days. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may file a complaint with the Commission d'acces a l'information du Quebec (cai.gouv.qc.ca).
